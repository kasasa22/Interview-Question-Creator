How has access to electricity improved globally, and what are the implications of continuing to rely on fossil fuels for energy?\nAnswer: Access to electricity has improved globally between 1990 and 2010, with 1.7 billion more people gaining access to electricity during that time. This progress is significant and has helped improve the quality of life for many individuals. However, continuing to rely on fossil fuels for energy has serious implications for the environment. Fossil fuels contribute to greenhouse gas emissions, which are a major driver of climate change. This can lead to drastic changes in the climate, causing problems such as extreme weather events, rising sea levels, and disruptions to ecosystems. To address these challenges, it is important to invest in clean energy sources such as solar and wind power, which can meet electricity needs while also protecting the environment.\n--------------------------------------------------\n\nQuestion: 10.
